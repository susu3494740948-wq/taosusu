# Cross-Border MVP Traffic Test Plan

## Channel Decision

Use two channels only for the first 14 days after launch:

1. TikTok organic content for product proof and audience signal.
2. Small-budget Meta or TikTok paid ads for controlled traffic testing.

Do not run Meta, TikTok, Google Shopping, influencer outreach, SEO, and email campaigns all at once in the first test. The goal is to isolate what is working.

## Budget Guardrails

Suggested first paid test budget: USD 20-50 per day for 7-10 days.

Stop or revise an ad set when:

- CTR is weak after enough impressions.
- Product page views do not become add-to-carts.
- Add-to-carts do not become checkouts.
- Checkout starts do not become purchases.
- Comments show confusion about price, shipping, product use, or trust.

Do not scale spend until tracking events are verified and at least one product shows purchase or strong add-to-cart signal.

## UTM Naming Convention

Use one consistent naming format:

`utm_source={platform}&utm_medium={organic|paid}&utm_campaign={sku_phase}&utm_content={creative_angle_date}`

Examples:

- `utm_source=tiktok&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=cooling_towel_mvp&utm_content=wet_snap_demo_2026-07-01`
- `utm_source=meta&utm_medium=paid&utm_campaign=pet_hair_remover_mvp&utm_content=sofa_before_after_2026-07-01`

## Daily Operating Rhythm

Morning:

- Check previous day spend, clicks, add-to-carts, checkouts, purchases, refund messages, and comments.
- Pause obvious losers.
- Write down the suspected bottleneck.

Midday:

- Publish or schedule 1-3 organic short videos.
- Reply to comments with product facts, shipping clarity, and use cases.

Evening:

- Add one new creative variation only if the prior test has enough signal.
- Update the tracker.
- Save winning comments and objections for product page FAQ improvements.

## Funnel Diagnosis

Use this simple diagnosis:

- Impressions but low clicks: creative hook or audience is weak.
- Clicks but low add-to-cart: product page, price, offer, or product-market fit is weak.
- Add-to-cart but low checkout: shipping, discount, trust, or cart experience is weak.
- Checkout but low purchase: payment options, final cost, trust, or policy clarity is weak.
- Purchase but refund/complaint: product quality, shipping promise, or expectation mismatch is weak.

## 14-Day Test Schedule

| Day | Action | Output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| D1 | Verify pixels and run internal test order | Purchase event confirmed |
| D2 | Publish 3 organic product demos | Baseline content engagement |
| D3 | Launch first paid test for 2 SKUs | Paid traffic starts |
| D4 | Review CTR and comments | Pause unclear creatives |
| D5 | Launch 2 new creative angles | Compare hooks |
| D6 | Update product page FAQ from comments | Trust objections addressed |
| D7 | First checkpoint | Keep, revise, or pause each SKU |
| D8-D10 | Continue winners and add variants | More stable funnel data |
| D11-D12 | Test offer changes only if add-to-cart exists | Price/bundle signal |
| D13 | Prepare decision summary | Go/Pivot/Stop draft |
| D14 | Final test review | Decide next 14-day action |
