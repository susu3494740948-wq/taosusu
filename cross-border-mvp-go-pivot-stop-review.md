# Cross-Border MVP Go / Pivot / Stop Review

Use this review at the end of week 7-8, or earlier if paid traffic has enough signal.

## Required Inputs

Collect these before making the decision:

- Total traffic by channel.
- Total ad spend.
- Product page views.
- Add-to-cart count and rate.
- Checkout starts and rate.
- Purchases and conversion rate.
- Revenue and average order value.
- Estimated contribution margin after product cost, fulfillment, payment fees, affiliate/creator fees, refunds, and ad spend.
- Refunds, complaints, chargebacks, and customer objections.
- Supplier reliability and restock lead time.
- Shipping time and tracking reliability.

## Decision Rules

### Go

Continue and invest another 14-30 days when most of these are true:

- At least one SKU has real purchases or strong add-to-cart signal.
- Contribution margin is positive before or near breakeven after ad spend.
- Customers understand the product without heavy explanation.
- Shipping and supplier quality are controllable.
- Refund reasons are not concentrated around product defects or misleading claims.
- At least one creative angle can be repeated or varied.

Next action after Go:

- Improve the winning product page.
- Order limited restock only for the winning SKU.
- Create 5-10 new creative variations around the winning angle.
- Add email capture and abandoned checkout recovery.
- Consider one bundle or quantity discount.

### Pivot

Revise product, offer, page, or traffic channel when any of these are true:

- Clicks exist but add-to-cart is weak.
- Add-to-cart exists but checkout or payment completion is weak.
- Comments show interest but buyers question trust, price, shipping, sizing, or use case.
- One product gets attention but economics are weak.
- Supplier or logistics issue blocks the current offer, but demand signal exists.

Next action after Pivot:

- Fix the narrowest bottleneck first.
- If page problem: improve product visuals, FAQ, trust, shipping clarity, and offer.
- If price problem: test bundle, free shipping threshold, or discount.
- If traffic problem: change creative hook or audience, not the whole site.
- If product problem: replace the SKU but keep the proven audience insight.

### Stop

Stop or change category when most of these are true:

- Multiple creatives and channels generate no meaningful product page engagement.
- Product page views do not become add-to-carts after clear page improvements.
- Supplier, compliance, or logistics risk cannot be solved within MVP budget.
- Gross margin cannot support ads, refunds, and support cost.
- Customer objections show weak product desirability, not just weak presentation.

Next action after Stop:

- Archive learnings.
- Keep reusable site structure, policies, pixels, and tracker.
- Pick a new SKU using the same scorecard.
- Do not keep spending just because the store is already built.

## Threshold Guide

These are not universal benchmarks, but they prevent emotional decisions:

- Product page add-to-cart rate below 2% after qualified traffic: revise product page, offer, or product.
- Checkout-start-to-purchase rate below 30%: inspect payment, shipping cost, final price, and trust.
- Refund/complaint rate above 8-10% in early orders: inspect product quality and expectation mismatch.
- Contribution margin below 25% before ad spend: do not scale paid ads.
- No meaningful product page engagement after 5-8 credible creatives: change creative angle or product.
